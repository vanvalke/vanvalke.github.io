Ethan VanValkenburg

Hello! I am a Ph.D. student at the Stanford University's Department of Biology in the Community Ecology lab of Tadashi Fukami.

I study mutualisms, ecological networks, plant-pollinator interactions, and global change ecology. I aspire to integrate natural history, field work, theory, computer modeling, and complex systems approaches to understand how ecological communities respond to changing environment.

At the University of Michigan Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, I studied the role of nectar traits in plant-pollinator interactions at The RMBL. I also studied the biogeography and dietary ecology of mammals and marsh bird species occurrence in Great Lakes Coastal Wetlands near the UM Biological Station. I also studied regenerative, perennial agriculture at The Land Institute.
