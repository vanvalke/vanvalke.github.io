Ethan VanValkenburg

Hello! I am a Ph.D. student at the Stanford University's Department of Biology in the Community Ecology lab of Tadashi Fukami.

I study mutualisms, ecological networks, plant-pollinator interactions, and global change ecology. I aspire to incorporate natural history, field work, theory, microcosm experiments, and complex systems approaches to understand how ecological communities respond to changing environments. I'm also eager to learn more about traditional ecological knowledge, cartography, and ways to incorporate science and art.

I did my undergraduate studies at the University of Michigan Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology. My thesis explored the role of nectar traits in plant-pollinator interactions at The RMBL. I have loved my experiences in work, research, and teaching at the UM Biological Station, The Land Institute, Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program, and Rocky Mountain Biological Laboratory.
